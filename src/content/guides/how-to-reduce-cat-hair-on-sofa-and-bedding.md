## Solve The Problem In The Right Order

People usually start by fighting the sofa. That is understandable, but it works better to reduce the loose fur at the source first. A brushing routine, a washable throw, and a quick cleanup tool usually outperform occasional deep cleaning.

## Start With The Cat

Brush often enough to catch loose hair before it spreads into the room. For many short-haired cats, one to two weekly sessions help. For heavier shedders, increase frequency during peak shedding periods.

## Protect The Main Surfaces

If one part of the sofa or bed attracts the cat every day, protect that area with an easy-to-wash layer. A throw blanket or cover is much easier to reset than cleaning the full surface each time.

## Make Daily Cleanup Lightweight

Keep one lint roller, rubber brush, or fabric tool near the place where hair builds up fastest. Fast access matters. If cleanup requires opening a closet and assembling equipment, most people delay it until the mess feels bigger.
