## Think About Reset Speed

In a small apartment, a mat that traps litter well but is annoying to clean can become its own problem. Fast reset matters. If the mat is hard to empty, hard to wash, or awkward around doors and corners, people stop maintaining it.

## Match The Mat To The Exit Pattern

Some cats hop out in one direction. Others step sideways or jump over the mat entirely. Watch how the cat actually exits the box before deciding whether you need a longer mat, a wider mat, or a different box placement.

## Prioritize Easy Cleaning

For everyday homes, washable or easy-shake surfaces usually beat complicated layered designs unless the tracking problem is severe. The simpler the cleanup, the more likely the system will hold up over time.

## Keep It Proportional To The Room

A mat that is too large can create visual clutter in a small home. A better target is a mat that catches most of the litter while still leaving the room easy to walk through and easy to mop.
